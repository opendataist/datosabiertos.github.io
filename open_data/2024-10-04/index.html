Costin Leading the Way for National BIM Study
The University of Florida s College of Design, Construction & Planning values innovation, with Dr. Aaron Costin leading a significant $2 million Department of Transportation study to expand building information modeling (BIM) for bridges and structures. BIM, a 3D modeling software process, digitally represents a facility s life cycle, enabling various applications and integration with emerging technologies like robots and AI. Costin s research aims to create an open data exchange standard for BIM specifically for bridges and other structures, which have been previously neglected. This project involves collaboration between the Federal Highway Administration, the American Association of State Highway Transportation Official Committee on Bridges and Structures, and 23 states, including Florida. Costin s role in the project includes leading traditional academic research, collecting industry knowledge, and developing guidelines for future implementation. The research will define the U.S. national standard and integrate work into similar projects worldwide, setting the international standard. Costin s work is inspired by his former advisor, Charles Chuck Eastman, known as the Father of BIM. Costin is honored to work in Eastman s legacy and believes the outcomes of the research will improve production and efficiency for different roles involved in building bridges. The success of this project will revolutionize transportation infrastructure construction and management, benefiting the economy and citizens. Overall, Costin is proud to be part of this groundbreaking research, which will serve as a starting point for future developments and applications in the field.

Microsoft announces Drasi , an open source Data Change Processing platform
Microsoft has introduced Drasi, an open-source Data Change Processing (DCP) platform, designed to automate intelligent reactions in dynamic systems. Drasi aims to streamline data processing by eliminating the need for traditional methods like copying data to centralized data lakes or periodic data source queries. Instead, it monitors for events in logs and change feeds to track data changes. Drasi s low-code query-based approach enables developers to create complex rules using rich graph queries, written in Cypher Query Language. These queries continuously evaluate incoming changes based on predefined criteria, integrating data from multiple sources without prior collation. The platform consists of three main components: 1. Sources: These connect to various data sources in systems, continuously monitoring for critical changes. Sources track application logs, database updates, or system metrics, gathering relevant information in real time. 2. Continuous Queries: Drasi uses Continuous Queries to constantly evaluate incoming changes based on predefined criteria. These queries are written in Cypher Query Language and can integrate data from multiple sources without needing prior collation. 3. Reactions: When changes complete a continuous query, Drasi executes registered automated reactions. These reactions can send alerts, update other systems, or perform remediation steps, tailored to the operational needs. Drasi supports customization and currently supports PostgreSQL, Microsoft Dataverse, and Azure Event Grid Sources and Reactions. Organizations can build their own integrations based on their needs. Drasi is available under the Apache 2.0 license, allowing its use in both commercial and non-commercial projects. In summary, Drasi is a new open-source DCP platform that automates intelligent reactions in dynamic systems by tracking data changes in real-time. It offers a low-code query-based approach, utilizing Cypher Query Language, and consists of three main components: Sources, Continuous Queries, and Reactions. The platform supports customization and is available under the Apache 2.0 license.

Wikimania 2024 : Wikipedia and Wikidata as sources for analyzing Americanization ( video )
At the Wikimania 2024 conference, researchers Prof. Piotr Konieczny and Dr. Włodzimierz Lewoniewski presented their findings on Americanization across different global regions using open data from Wikipedia and Wikidata. The study revealed varying levels of interest in American topics, correlating with the development stages of countries. This research highlights the significance of Americanization as a subject of scholarly inquiry and showcases the potential of online platforms like Wikipedia and Wikidata for social science research. The study also draws attention to the uneven distribution of content related to American topics across different language versions of Wikipedia. This discrepancy raises concerns about the global representation of knowledge and the inherent biases present in the platform. The researchers emphasize the need for a more equitable and inclusive approach to content creation and editing on Wikipedia to ensure that the encyclopedia remains a balanced and comprehensive resource for users worldwide. The presentation and additional materials can be found on the Wikimania website (wikimania2024.lewoniewski.info). Wikimania is an annual conference that brings together volunteers and Free Knowledge leaders from around the world to discuss open-access knowledge projects run by the Wikimedia Foundation. The 2024 edition took place in Katowice, Poland, with over 2,000 participants from 143 countries. The conference website is wikimania.wikimedia.org. In summary, the research presented at Wikimania 2024 sheds light on the phenomenon of Americanization and its implications for global cultural dominance. The study also highlights the methodological potential of using online platforms like Wikipedia and Wikidata for social science research, while calling for a more inclusive approach to content creation and editing on these platforms.
