Harnessing the Power of Data: From Africa to North America, Open Data Drives Innovation and Social Change

Published: 2023-08-09

Today’s news digest brings you a roundup of the latest developments in the world of open data. From Africa to North America, companies and organizations are harnessing the power of data to drive innovation, address social issues, and create a more transparent and accessible future.

In Africa, Lelapa AI is taking a stand against “digital colonialism” by developing AI systems designed specifically for African needs. By building algorithms tailored to under-represented African languages, Lelapa AI aims to address the concerns of exploitation and ensure equitable benefits for local communities. The company’s open data platform documenting the restitution process of African heritage objects further promotes transparency and knowledge sharing.

Meanwhile, Nuvei Corporation is revolutionizing the fintech industry with its comprehensive payment solutions. By supporting over 634 alternative payment methods and 150 currencies, Nuvei enables merchants to accept payments from anywhere in the world. Their data-driven business intelligence tools and risk management services provide valuable insights and statistics to help businesses make informed decisions.

In Kansas City, the 311 program is shedding light on the issues affecting residents. Trash-related reports and property violations are among the top concerns, with certain districts experiencing higher volumes and longer resolution times. The city is actively exploring ways to improve language capabilities and address the challenges faced by immigrants and refugees in navigating the 311 process. By making data more accessible and providing informational resources, Kansas City aims to create a more inclusive and responsive community.

Strider Technologies is tackling the issue of intellectual property protection with its risk intelligence solution, Sentry. By leveraging data and advanced AI processing, organizations can identify potential connections to state-sponsored risk and safeguard their technology and talent. Strider’s suite of strategic intelligence products aims to secure IP, technology, talent, and supply chains, ensuring the integrity and transparency of critical assets.

The National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) recently hosted a workshop to envision sustainable mobility systems of the future. By bringing together industry leaders, government officials, and academics, NREL aims to identify the technologies and trends that will shape energy-efficient and equitable transportation solutions. The workshop emphasized the importance of data sharing and communication in implementing sustainable mobility initiatives.

OpenStreetMap (OSM), the largest open-source geospatial project, is celebrating its 19th anniversary and launching a fundraising campaign to support its future. OSM’s global community of volunteers collaborates in multiple languages to provide accurate and up-to-date geospatial data. The campaign encourages individuals to contribute to the map and ensure the continued success of this valuable resource.

In an interview with Nick Clegg, President of Global Affairs at Meta, the regulation of artificial intelligence (AI) takes center stage. Clegg emphasizes the need for a balanced approach that considers both innovation and regulation. He highlights Meta’s commitment to open-source AI models and addresses concerns about data privacy and content moderation. Clegg’s insights shed light on the ongoing debates surrounding AI and its impact on society.

These stories highlight the power of data, information, and knowledge in driving positive change. From Africa to North America, companies and organizations are leveraging open data to address social issues, promote transparency, and create a more accessible and equitable future. By harnessing the potential of data, we can leave a better world for future generations.
