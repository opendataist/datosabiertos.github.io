Staten Island Speeding Violations: Analyzing Data to Improve Road Safety

Published: 2023-12-17

Staten Island has a lot of violations because drivers go faster than they should. From January to September 2023, there were 312,084 violations, and the fines added up to $15,604,200.

The cameras that check speed on Staten Island work all the time and take pictures when people go too fast. This information helps us understand how people drive. By looking at this information, the people in charge can find places where a lot of people speed and do something about it.

The top 20 places with the most violations and fines have been found. The place with the most violations is Westbound Goethals Road North @ Jules Drive. There were 29,316 violations there, and the fines added up to $1,465,800. This shows that we need to do more to make people drive safely in that area.

Having data that is open to the public is very important. It helps people see how many times people break the speed limit on Staten Island. This lets people hold the people in charge responsible and ask for safer roads.

Having information about the violations lets researchers and people who make rules study it. They can find patterns and trends in the data. This helps them make plans to make people drive slower and make the roads safer.

Also, when people pay the fines for the violations, it helps the city get money. This shows that it is important to make sure people follow the traffic laws because it affects the local economy.

In conclusion, the cameras that check speed on Staten Island have found a lot of violations and fines. This information is important for the people in charge, researchers, and the public. By using open data and being honest, we can make the roads safer and have a better future for our kids.
