The Transformative Power of Open Data: From City Governance to AI Development

Published: 2023-07-12

Open data is transforming the way we interact with our cities and governments, making information more accessible and transparent. Today, we bring you a roundup of news stories that highlight the power of open data in various contexts.

In Bangalore, Open City, a civic tech platform, is organizing a data jam to analyze the work orders, finances, and processes of the local municipal body, BBMP. This event aims to collect and analyze open data related to BBMP’s finances and processes, identify challenges, and propose solutions. By making data more accessible and understandable, the data jam aims to bridge the gap between the general public and the local government. This event is open to the public and requires an interest in solving the city’s problems, with skills in data analysis, visualization, coding, civic works, or urban planning being helpful.

In Kathmandu, three bills have been presented in the Municipal Assembly. These bills focus on communication and information, public health services, and the organization of the metropolitan police. The Communication and Information Bill aims to ensure communication management, data collection, and freedom of thought and expression. The Public Health Service Bill aims to make free, basic, and emergency healthcare services accessible to citizens. The Metropolitan Police Bill aims to organize the mobilization of city police for the effective implementation of KMC’s policies, laws, and decisions. These bills highlight the importance of open data in shaping policies and providing essential services to the public.

In Norway, generative artificial intelligence (AI) has raised concerns about data privacy and potential infringements on the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The AI systems, which can generate near-human content, collect personal data from various sources, potentially violating the principle of data minimization. The Norwegian Consumer Council calls for strict enforcement of consumer protection laws and adequate resources for regulatory agencies to address these concerns. This highlights the need for responsible AI development and the importance of protecting personal data.

In Africa, Niyel, an impact-driven campaign and advocacy firm, played a key role in organizing the AfricAI Conference 2023. This conference focused on advancing responsible and open AI ecosystems in Africa. Stakeholders highlighted the importance of human input in training AI and discussed inclusive policies, research and development, open data initiatives, and cross-sector collaborations. Niyel aims to address policy challenges using data-based evidence and partnerships with research institutions. This conference showcases the potential of open data in shaping the future of AI in Africa.

On a different note, Veeva Systems Inc., a cloud-based software provider for the life sciences industry, offers a range of solutions that leverage data analytics and management. Their applications cater to various functions within the industry, including customer relationship management, content and digital asset management, clinical and regulatory functions, and quality and safety functions. This highlights the role of data analytics in improving efficiency and decision-making in the life sciences industry.

In conclusion, open data is driving positive change in various sectors, from urban planning to healthcare to AI development. By making data more accessible and transparent, we can empower citizens, shape policies, and foster innovation. These stories demonstrate the power of open data in creating a more inclusive and informed society.
