Innovative Data Solutions Transform Cloud Billing, Connectivity, and Financial Services

Published: 2024-06-20

Welcome to today’s Daily News Digest! Let’s dive into the world of open data and see how it changes our lives. Understanding open data is important. Let’s look at the latest news and how it makes our future brighter.

The FinOps Foundation has introduced the FinOps Open Cost and Usage Specification (FOCUS) Version 1.0. This new system standardizes cloud billing for big providers like Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure, AWS, and Oracle Cloud. This helps businesses understand and manage their cloud costs better. With FOCUS, companies can look at discounts, share costs, and get the most out of their investments.

Smart Communications, Inc. has launched Magic Apps. This offers NO-EXPIRY data for popular platforms like Facebook and YouTube. For just P99, users get 3 GB of data that never expires. This is great for social networking, video streaming, and occasional data use. Magic Apps, powered by Smart’s award-winning network, ensures a smooth experience for everyone.

T-Mobile and SailGP have teamed up to bring a thrilling 5G experience to fans at the Mubadala New York Sail Grand Prix. T-Mobile’s advanced 5G network provides real-time data, better fan engagement, and dynamic race views. This technology ensures important data is sent instantly, making the spectator experience better both on-site and at home.

Oracle’s Autonomous Database is now available on Oracle Database@Azure in the Microsoft Azure East U.S. region. This service speeds up application innovation and cloud migration with fully automated and managed services. With features like Oracle APEX, Data Studio, and AI capabilities, developers can build new solutions easily. Customers enjoy high performance, scalability, and security while staying compatible with Azure technologies.

Visual Matrix has joined forces with Hapi to integrate property-level data into the Hapi Integration Platform. This improves connectivity for hotel brands and Salesforce CRM capabilities. Hotels can access comprehensive data through a single API, creating personalized guest experiences and boosting bookings. Sonesta International Hotels is already benefiting, streamlining data across their portfolio for better guest satisfaction.

NATO data shows that 24 member states will meet or exceed the 2% GDP investment in defense, the highest number since 2014. Total NATO spending in 2024 is expected to be $1.5 trillion, a 12% increase from 2023. However, the U.S. is disappointed as eight states fall short of the 2% goal. This data highlights the importance of collective defense efforts and balanced contributions.

The FAST 50 2025 report highlights Murrumba Downs, a suburb near Brisbane, as a promising area for growth. With affordable homes, waterfront properties, schools, and recreational areas, it is an attractive option for buyers and investors. The suburb’s median house price is below Brisbane’s average, making it a good choice for those seeking value and community.

FinTech companies are changing financial services with easier user experiences in data aggregation, lending, and mutual funds. Collaboration between FinTechs and traditional financial institutions is important to meet the growing demand for different financial products while ensuring regulatory compliance. The rise of open data ecosystems and better cybersecurity measures are driving this change, with product-led SaaS solutions gaining popularity in lending and investment sectors.

WIOCC Group, a leading digital infrastructure provider in Africa, has received funding from IFC and Proparco to expand in the Democratic Republic of Congo, Nigeria, and South Africa. This funding supports the growth of data centers and fiber networks, fostering economic development and bridging the digital divide. The expansion aims to create a connected digital ecosystem across key African markets, enhancing digital connectivity and closing the digital gap.

As we continue to embrace open data and its many uses, we pave the way for a more informed, connected, and fair world. Stay tuned for more updates and insights on how open data is shaping our future.

Thank you for joining us in today’s exploration of open data. Together, we can use information, knowledge, and insights to create a better world for our children and future generations.
