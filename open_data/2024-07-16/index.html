"How Open Data is Transforming Defense, Finance, Mobility, and Scientific Research"

Published: 2024-07-16

Welcome to today’s Daily Open Data Digest! We bring you the latest news on open data and how it changes our world. Today’s stories show how open data helps innovation, transparency, and fairness in many areas. Let’s get started!

The Department of Defense (DoD) recently held a big event called the Global Information Dominance Experiments (GIDE) Industry Insights Forum. This event showed how important it is for industries and the government to work together on artificial intelligence (AI). They introduced a new program called Open Data and Applications Government-owned Interoperable Repositories (Open DAGIR). This program aims to get industries more involved and speed up innovation in important DoD projects. It shows how teamwork can make our country safer with AI.

A company named 9Spokes has launched a new product to help small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs) manage their cash flow. This tool uses smart machine-learning algorithms to predict cash flow by gathering data from different sources. It fits into the global trend of open banking, making financial tasks easier and giving SMBs useful insights. This product can change how SMBs handle money and make decisions, offering a complete financial overview and even allowing banks to use it under their own brands.

Namma Yatri, a community-led mobility app, has raised $11 million to improve its technology. With 7 million users and 400,000 drivers in eight cities, the app wants to create a clear and sustainable transportation system. Co-founders Magizhan Selvan and Shan MS focus on making products that put people first and improve user experience. Their goal is to become the “UPI for mobility,” changing the transportation sector.

In an effort to save scientific knowledge, digital data from CERN’s old LEP accelerator is being made available through digital archaeology projects. Teams are updating old software to work with modern tools, making sure valuable scientific data is available for future research. Data from the ALEPH and DELPHI experiments can now be found on the CERN Open Data Portal, allowing researchers, students, and enthusiasts to explore electron-positron annihilation data.

The ELLAS project is working to fix gender gaps in STEM leadership in Latin America by creating an open data platform. This platform collects data from different sources, models it in many languages, and uses knowledge graphs for easy access. By involving stakeholders and using human-centered design, the project aims to provide reliable data to address underrepresentation and promote economic and social fairness.

In Kenya, anti-government protests have grown due to the controversial Finance Bill 2024. President William Ruto has accused foreign groups like the Ford Foundation of funding the protests, although they deny it. The Ford Foundation, active in Kenya since 1962, supports civic space and social justice issues. This highlights the ongoing fight for social justice and the role of open data in promoting transparency and accountability.

Africa Hyperscalers Media has announced the first Hyperscalers Convergence Africa conference in Lagos, set for September 5, 2024. The event aims to bring together Africa’s digital infrastructure community to foster collaboration and innovation in data centers, cloud services, and related sectors. With the theme “The Power of Convergence,” the conference will explore new technologies shaping Africa’s digital future, focusing on sustainability and strong digital infrastructure.

A recent podcast episode discusses the impact of artificial intelligence (AI) on intellectual property (IP) rights, focusing on AI’s ability to mimic human traits. The talk highlights the right of publicity, which protects people from having their likeness used without permission. As AI evolves, the ethical and legal challenges of copying human traits become more important, showing the need to protect personal rights in the digital age.

The Aurora Fire Department has launched a new public website, the Aurora Fire Department Open Data Portal. This site provides residents with key information on fire prevention and emergency management. The portal offers statistics on carbon monoxide calls, community events, and more, enhancing transparency and helping residents better serve and protect their community.

Veeva Systems’ stock performance has seen a recent change, with Piper Sandler lowering its price target to $225.00 while keeping an overweight rating. The company, known for its cloud-based software for the life sciences industry, continues to attract attention from investors and analysts, showing its important role in the sector.

That’s all for today’s edition of the Daily Open Data Digest. Stay tuned for more updates on how open data drives change and makes a positive impact on our world. Together, we can use the power of information to create a better future for all.
