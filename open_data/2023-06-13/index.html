The Future of AI, Smart Cities, and Open Data: Daily Digest

Published: 2023-06-13

Artificial intelligence (AI) innovators are shaping the future of AI technology with their unique perspectives and opinions. Daniela Amodei, co-founder of Anthropic, emphasizes the need for regulation in the AI field. Yann LeCun, chief AI scientist at Meta, believes AI will amplify human intelligence rather than replace it. David Luan, CEO of Adept, believes AI will enhance the work of knowledge workers. Emad Mostaque, CEO of Stability AI, explores the potential impact and risks of AI.

IoT and AI are playing a crucial role in the development of smart cities. These technologies connect and monitor various aspects of urban life, improving transportation, energy, and public services. Despite challenges such as cost, data prioritization, automation, and data security, smart cities offer benefits like improved quality of life and enhanced commercial opportunities.

Grafana Labs has launched Grafana v10, an open-source data visualization platform. The update focuses on improving the developer experience, with new visualization panels, an improved onboarding process, and tools for richer dashboards. It also introduces hardened security measures, making data more accessible and transparent.

Research conducted by the Finnish Museum of Natural History has determined the most visually appealing bird species based on human perception. The study scored the aesthetic attractiveness of over 10,000 bird species globally, revealing that colorful birds and large birds of prey ranked high. This research has implications for conservation activities and nature tourism.

The US Geological Survey (USGS) has released a large amount of Mars data, democratizing the availability of high-quality topographic data. The data includes digital terrain models and ultra-high-resolution images of the Martian surface, allowing scientists and the public to view the planet at high resolution. This data opens up opportunities for scientific discovery and contributes to our knowledge of Mars.

Marine telemetry, tracking marine wildlife using acoustic and satellite tags, is vital for understanding the impacts of climate change on oceans and biodiversity. Telemetry is used to track species movement, monitor marine protected areas, and gather environmental data. Collaborative efforts between tracking networks enhance transparency and access to valuable datasets.

Trinity College Dublin researcher Colm Delaney is creating photonic sensors using ‘structural colour’ found in birds, insects, and chameleons. These sensors have applications in medical and environmental monitoring. Delaney emphasizes the importance of funding fundamental research and public engagement in science, especially in the context of open access data.

Wego, the largest online travel marketplace in the Middle East and North Africa, has partnered with the German National Tourist Office for a summer campaign promoting sustainability, nature, and culture in Germany. The campaign aims to increase the number of travelers and bookings to Germany, highlighting the country’s sustainable tourism practices and diverse culture.

Accurate data is crucial for addressing broadband internet issues in Pennsylvania. Decision-makers should use accurate data to determine areas that require federal funding. The federal government has allocated funding for broadband adoption, and Pennsylvania is likely to receive a significant amount. Equitable distribution of funding is essential for achieving universal broadband adoption.

Altron Karabina, a leading Microsoft data partner in South Africa, has partnered with Databricks to offer Azure Databricks services. This partnership allows Altron Karabina to provide its customers with access to the power and functionality of Databricks. Databricks is confident that Altron Karabina will expand its reach in the markets where it operates.

BAM Digital Realty is investing in a data center in Mumbai, India, to meet the rising demand for data centers in the country. The data center will have a capacity of 35 MW IT load and will support India’s aspirations as a digitally driven connectivity hub.

Royal Caribbean Group has extended its investment in OceanScope, an open-source data program that provides critical information for studying climate and ocean conservation. The program collects data on the ocean’s vital signs and is available to the international scientific research community. Royal Caribbean Group is committed to sustainability and aims to reach net-zero emissions by 2050.

Business intelligence (BI) has transformed Ghana’s insurance sector, enabling insurers to make informed decisions and improve efficiency. BI tools have enabled efficient reporting, data visualization, and predictive analytics. The future of BI in Ghana’s insurance sector includes advancements in AI and personalized, real-time services.

Equinix Inc plans to open a data center in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, to support the country’s aspirations as a digitally driven connectivity hub. The expansion aims to meet the rising demand for data centers driven by cloud growth, eCommerce adoption, and investment in 5G.

They highlight the innovative use of technology, the importance of accurate data, and the potential benefits of open access to information. By staying informed about these developments, we can contribute to a more accessible and transparent world.
