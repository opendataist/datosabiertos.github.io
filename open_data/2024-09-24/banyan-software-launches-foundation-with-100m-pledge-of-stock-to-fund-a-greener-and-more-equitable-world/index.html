Banyan Software Launches Foundation With $100M Pledge of Stock to Fund a Greener and More Equitable World

Banyan Software, a prominent acquirer of enterprise software businesses, has announced the establishment of the Banyan Software Foundation. The Foundation aims to support organizations utilizing technology to foster a more sustainable and equitable world. With a $100 million stock commitment over four years, the Foundation will become one of Banyan s largest shareholders, with its influence growing alongside the company s success. The Foundation s first two grant recipients, TransitionZero and Merit America, address pressing global challenges. TransitionZero, based in the UK, develops open-source data and software products for energy systems modeling, aiding low-income countries in their energy transition efforts. Merit America, a US-based nonprofit, offers online coursework and personalized coaching to rapidly re-skill low-wage workers for family-sustaining careers. The grant from the Banyan Software Foundation will support TransitionZero s Solar Asset Mapper project, a crucial tool for grid operators and analysts to manage the increasing prevalence of solar energy in the global energy landscape. This initiative will empower countries to pursue sustainable energy solutions independently. In addition to the Foundation, Banyan Software has introduced several internal purpose-driven initiatives, including dedicated funds for employee learning and skills advancement, an Employee Relief Fund for those experiencing extreme hardship, and a volunteer time off program. Banyan Software, founded in 2016, follows a buy-and-hold-for-life strategy and is committed to making a lasting, positive impact on the world. The company has offices across the US, Canada, the UK, EU, and APAC, and is one of North America s fastest-growing private businesses. For more information on Banyan s impact initiatives, visit banyansoftware.com/impact.
