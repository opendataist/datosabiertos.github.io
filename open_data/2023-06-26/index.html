Using Open Source Methods to Hold Russian Soldiers Accountable for War Crimes in Ukraine

Published: 2023-06-26

Investigators from Bellingcat and the Global Legal Action Network (GLAN) are using open source methods to hold Russian soldiers and military leaders accountable for war crimes committed in Ukraine. By utilizing social media posts and videos, these investigators are changing the way war crimes are documented and the structure of the legal system. Despite challenges in introducing digital evidence in court and gaining trust in internet content, progress is being made in getting findings into courtrooms and potentially bringing war criminals to justice.

OS Hub, formerly known as the Open Apparel Registry, has announced new appointments to its board of directors. This organization aims to make supply chain data open and accessible, and has seen increased interest in its open data approach from sectors such as food and beverage, sporting goods, electronics, and toys. With supply chain legislations being enacted globally, there is a growing emphasis on supply chain mapping. The new board members, with diverse backgrounds and global representation, will help shape the strategic direction of OS Hub and ensure its technology is a critical piece of infrastructure for global supply chains.

Flooding in Europe is expected to worsen in the future due to climate change and socio-economic factors. A study has assessed the risk of river-based flooding in Europe and estimated flood losses to homes. The study provides valuable information for policymakers and states in planning flood adaptation strategies.

The American Academy of Pediatrics has released a clinical report addressing controversies in treating inguinal hernias in children. The report recommends deferring hernia repair until after discharge from the neonatal intensive care unit and suggests that a laparoscopic approach is as effective, if not better, than the traditional open high ligation method. The report also discusses the debate on contralateral exploration in patients with unilateral hernias.

Google CEO Sundar Pichai has pledged $20 million to support and expand the Consortium of Cybersecurity Clinics, aiming to introduce thousands of students to potential careers in cybersecurity. The APEC Business Advisory Council (ABAC) is advocating for the interoperability of central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) across all 21 member economies. This would allow for wholesale settlements without relying on the US payment system. Interoperable CBDCs would make settlements faster and more cost-effective. Various CBDC initiatives are already underway in the APEC region, and the US is now actively participating in the interoperability proposal.
