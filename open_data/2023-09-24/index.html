Lawmakers Call for Bipartisan Action to Address Shelter System and Workforce Shortage in Massachusetts

More than 60 lawmakers from Massachusetts are calling for a bipartisan solution to address the state’s emergency shelter system and workforce shortage. They argue that the combination of homeless families and newly arriving immigrants has pushed the shelter system to its limits. This issue highlights the ongoing labor shortages in various sectors of the US economy and the need for immigration reform to provide a practical means for noncitizens to find employment.

The lawmakers are emphasizing the economic benefits of providing a pathway to citizenship for eligible and undocumented workers. They recognize that these workers contribute to the economy and play a vital role in filling labor gaps. By providing a pathway to citizenship, these workers would have access to better job opportunities, leading to increased economic growth and stability.
