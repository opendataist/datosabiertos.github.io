Meta Platforms Inc Launches Code Llama: A Language Model for Code Generation and Discussion

Published: 2023-08-25

Meta Platforms Inc, the parent company of Facebook, has recently launched Code Llama, a large language model (LLM) designed for generating and discussing code. This new tool, built on top of the Llama 2 model, is available for both research and commercial use. Code Llama supports popular programming languages and can be used for code completion and debugging. It has been trained with a massive amount of code and code-related data, making it a valuable resource for developers.

However, it’s important to note that Code Llama may sometimes provide inaccurate responses, so users should exercise caution when relying on its suggestions. Despite this limitation, the launch of Code Llama demonstrates Meta’s commitment to advancing AI technology and providing developers with innovative tools to enhance their coding experience.

In other news, a bakery in Toronto, Alminz Bakeshop, has been flagged by health inspectors for multiple infractions. The bakery received a conditional pass notice after inspectors found 10 infractions, including crucial, significant, and minor violations. These infractions ranged from improper handwashing practices to inadequate pest control measures. While the bakery will remain open, the staff is working diligently to address these issues and ensure compliance with health and safety regulations.

Shifting our focus to road safety, the city of Ottawa has implemented an automated speed enforcement camera program to make roads safer. In the first six months of 2023, one of the city’s newest photo radar cameras on Fisher Avenue issued a staggering 11,072 speeding tickets. Overall, the city’s 20 automated speed enforcement cameras issued a total of 63,440 tickets during this period, generating $10.7 million in revenue. Ottawa plans to install an additional 20 cameras this year, bringing the total to 40 by the end of 2023. This program aims to promote safer driving habits and support Ottawa’s Road Safety Action Plan.

Lastly, India has made history by becoming the fourth country to achieve a soft landing on the Moon’s south pole region. This remarkable accomplishment was achieved with a budget of $74.58 million, showcasing India’s emergence as a space power. The country is now in discussions with Japan for a joint Moon mission, further solidifying its position in the global space exploration arena. However, neighboring Bangladesh’s space research institution, SPARRSO, is facing significant challenges. With limited infrastructure, technology, manpower, and leadership, Bangladesh lags behind in the space race. While India continues to make strides in space exploration, Bangladesh currently has no plans for space expeditions in the near future.
