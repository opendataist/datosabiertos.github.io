World Bank Approves $150 Million Loan for Resilient Kerala Program

Published: 2023-06-17

The World Bank has approved a $150 million loan to support the Resilient Kerala Program, aimed at strengthening the Indian state’s preparedness against natural disasters, climate change impacts, and disease outbreaks. This funding will complement the World Bank’s previous investment of $125 million and is expected to protect nearly 5 million people from the impacts of floods. The project will focus on addressing coastal erosion and water resource management, areas where Kerala is highly vulnerable. The loan will also help develop a climate budget and improve the state’s open data and digital systems to reduce vulnerability to natural hazards.

In Sierra Leone, Baroness Patricia Scotland, Secretary-General of the Commonwealth, visited Freetown to discuss collaboration and transforming the city with Mayor Yvonne Aki-Sawyerr. They also discussed the Commonwealth Secretariat’s support in promoting prosperity, development, democracy, and peace in Sierra Leone. Baroness Scotland highlighted the achievements of the Ministry of Finance and the Ministry of Planning and Economic Development and recommended the re-establishment of the Commonwealth Fund for Technical Cooperation. Sierra Leone expressed its commitment to upholding the values and principles of the Commonwealth.

Digital transformation is thriving in Da Nang, Vietnam, with the involvement of the entire political system, organizations, businesses, and residents. The city has built numerous databases and apps for public services, with citizens accessing them through common data repositories. Da Nang has also implemented a long-range transmission system, established a public WiFi network, and developed IoT infrastructure for data collection. The Da Nang Smart City mobile platform offers over 30 intelligent services, and there are approximately 260,000 digital citizen accounts in the city. This digital economy has contributed 17% to the city’s GDP, aiding in economic recovery and the development of a digital society.

In the defense industry, interoperable data standards are crucial for major defense companies to avoid becoming obsolete. The Pentagon’s former Chief Data Officer, David Spirk, emphasized the importance of interoperability in automated processes as defense companies seek to merge or partner with smaller tech firms. The defense industry recognizes the value of investing in innovation and establishing relationships with venture funds to gain a competitive edge in the market.

In the automotive industry, wider access to car data is a topic of debate. While the US Department of Transportation and some auto companies express concerns about security risks, experts argue that it is possible to create a safe data-sharing system through collaboration. In Massachusetts, conflicts between the state, the auto industry, and the federal government have led to restrictions on access to car data. This has frustrated car owners, but some companies stand by their commitment to consumer choice. The future of automotive repair and data access is now in the hands of the courts.

In Thailand, Advanced Info Service (AIS) has partnered with King Mongkut’s University of Technology Thonburi (KMUTT) to launch the Thailand Cyber Wellness Index (TCWI). This open data source measures digital skills nationwide and aims to provide an annual measurement of Thailand’s digital savvy. The index covers seven areas of digital skills and categorizes respondents into three levels: advanced, basic, and improvement. AIS aims to educate society on cyber threats and promote safe internet use.
