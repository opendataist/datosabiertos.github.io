Advancements in Open Data: Cloud Services, Energy Storage, Cybersecurity, and More

Published: 2023-09-15

The world of open data is constantly evolving, with new developments and initiatives taking place every day. Today, we bring you a roundup of the latest news in the open data space, covering a wide range of topics from cloud services to energy storage systems.

In Thailand, the Office of National Digital Economy and Society Committee (ONDE) is exploring ways to determine benchmark rental fees for state organizations to rent cloud services from private cloud providers. This move aims to facilitate the digital transformation of government agencies, as the current Government Data Centre and Cloud (GDCC) is unable to keep up with the high demand. By establishing a benchmark rate, state agencies will have the option to rent cloud services outside the GDCC, benefiting private cloud operators and promoting transparency in the process.

Meanwhile, in Switzerland, researchers at Empa are utilizing the Aurora battery robot to accelerate the development of new energy storage systems. This project is part of the Battery2030+ initiative, which recently received significant funding from the European Union. The Aurora robot platform automates and streamlines the material selection, assembly, and analysis of battery cells, with the goal of speeding up the development process. By leveraging machine learning, the Aurora AI can create mathematical models and determine which experiments should be conducted autonomously. This project not only promotes open research data but also has the potential to bring energy storage prototypes to market more efficiently.

In the cybersecurity realm, Amazon Web Services (AWS) and Cribl Inc. have joined forces to create the Amazon Security Lake. This purpose-built data lake centralizes security data across enterprises and standardizes it into a single logging format. This automation and increased visibility provide better outcomes for security teams, enabling more effective response and remediation. The partnership between AWS and Cribl aims to simplify the ingestion process, regardless of the source of the logs. The Security Lake is a game-changer in the industry, addressing the challenges of data growth and cost-effectiveness. It also offers potential for future data mining and AI integration, further enhancing security capabilities.

Moving on to financial applications, the eurofxref-hist.zip file serves as an open data interchange protocol widely used in the financial sector. Its availability as open data also functions as an open API, facilitating the exchange of data between organizations. This seamless data exchange promotes transparency and accessibility, enabling financial institutions to access the information they need efficiently.

Shifting our focus to the city of Cambridge, progress is being made in the Community Safety Department. The unarmed response team is set to start taking 911 calls in March, following a three-month period of community familiarization. The department’s distinctive T-shirts will help differentiate them from plainclothes officers, promoting transparency and building trust within the community. Additionally, the City Manager’s Office is actively addressing various initiatives, including zoning for the Alewife neighborhood and climate change mitigation. Discussions on topics such as transitioning to green lawn care equipment and implementing green roofs are also underway. The city’s commitment to open data is evident through the Open Data Review Board Meeting, which will focus on open data licensing and artificial intelligence.

Lastly, the African Development Bank and the Government of Korea have signed agreements worth $28.6 million to support Africa’s development agenda. The funding will primarily focus on energy access, agricultural transformation, and knowledge and capacity-building in African countries. This partnership aims to strengthen statistical cooperation and enhance the capacity of African countries to produce quality data, promoting transparency and informed decision-making.

In conclusion, the world of open data is vibrant and ever-changing, with new advancements and collaborations taking place across various sectors. From cloud services to energy storage systems, cybersecurity, financial applications, and community safety, open data is driving transparency, accessibility, and knowledge-sharing. These developments pave the way for a better future, where information and facts are readily available, empowering individuals and organizations to make informed decisions and create positive change.
