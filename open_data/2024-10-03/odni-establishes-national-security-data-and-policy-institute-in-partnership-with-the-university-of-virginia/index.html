ODNI Establishes National Security Data and Policy Institute in Partnership with the University of Virginia

The Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI) has announced a pioneering partnership with the University of Virginia (UVA) to establish the National Security Data and Policy Institute (NSDPI). This collaboration aims to advance the Intelligence Community s (IC) use of emerging technologies for data collection, analysis, and management, which is vital for the IC s foreign intelligence mission. Director of National Intelligence Avril Haines emphasized the importance of this partnership, highlighting the need for collaboration between the IC and academia to better understand national security issues. The partnership will help identify and leverage open-source data of utmost value to national security, while also sharing insights with industry partners to facilitate collaboration on critical national security matters. University of Virginia President Jim Ryan expressed gratitude for the ODNI s investment in UVA and their confidence in the university s research and leadership capabilities. The partnership aligns with the 2023 National Intelligence Strategy, which calls for the IC to work with a broad set of partners to enhance operational capabilities, enrich data sources, and sharpen insights in response to the increasingly complex and evolving global operating environment. The NSDPI will focus on addressing security and policy challenges facing the nation by capitalizing on academic strengths from across the University. This collaboration will enable the IC to better understand and respond to new threats, such as cybersecurity challenges, by utilizing advanced analytic tools and artificial intelligence. The ODNI s announcement of the NSDPI partnership with UVA marks a significant step in strengthening the Intelligence Community s ability to effectively utilize emerging technologies and open-source data to support national security objectives.
