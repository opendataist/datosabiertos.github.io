The Importance of Transparency and Responsible Technology in the Digital Age

Published: 2024-02-06

Understanding the Importance of Open Data and Transparency for Governments

In today’s digital age, money is changing with the introduction of central bank digital currencies (CBDCs). Fintech expert Sergei Kondratenko explains the benefits of CBDCs, including competition, financial inclusion, cost savings, and better regulation. However, there are concerns about privacy, cybersecurity, and disruption to the banking system. To make CBDCs work well, a reliable digital record is needed, with blockchain technology playing a key role. CBDCs have the potential to transform banking, speed up digitalization, and strengthen national currencies.

Making Government More Transparent

The lack of transparency in the UK government is worrying, as highlighted by Ed Humpherson, head of the Office for Statistics Regulation (OSR). Data is not always made public, with officials sometimes choosing not to release information or simply forgetting to do so. This lack of transparency affects how well the government performs and the country’s ranking in open data. The OSR is pushing for more consistency, honesty, and transparency across government departments. Humpherson says that evaluations of policy impact should be made public to improve trust.

Dealing with Challenges and Finding Solutions

The increase in artificial intelligence (AI) models has raised concerns about data privacy, the creation of fake content, and the amplification of biases. Ashley Casovan, a board member at the Responsible AI Institute, says that there needs to be rules for AI to reduce these risks. The AI Governance Center, part of the International Association of Privacy Professionals, is creating training and certification programs to tackle these challenges. Taking a careful approach and having an AI governance officer are important in making sure AI is used responsibly.

Improving User Experience Monitoring

IT departments are monitoring different parts of their systems, employees, and customers more and more. However, the way they do this can be better. Looking at the user’s experience as a whole, including all the things that affect it, is the answer. This approach focuses on gathering lots of information, using open standards, personalizing the experience, predicting and fixing problems, using normal language, and designing well. Admins need to think like designers, not just observers, to make sure users have a smooth experience.

Making Iraq More Connected

Sparkle, a company that helps with infrastructure and connections, has expanded its network to Iraq. They have opened a new point where they can connect to the internet in Erbil. This point, with the help of Novel Point, provides internet services to Iraq and nearby countries. This partnership aims to bring innovation, connections, and progress to Iraq, making it a center for digital excellence.

In Conclusion: Embracing Transparency and Open Data

Transparency and open data are very important in today’s digital world. Governments need to make data public to build trust and evaluate policies better. The challenges of new technologies like CBDCs and AI need rules to make sure they are used responsibly. Also, improving how we monitor user experiences and making sure more places have internet helps make the digital world fairer and easier for everyone. By being open and using data well, governments can make a better future for everyone.
