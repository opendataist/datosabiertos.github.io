"Mapping New York City's Urban Canopy: Tree Folio NYC Tool Calculates Shade Benefits"

In today’s news, researchers at Cornell University have developed an innovative tool called Tree Folio NYC, which creates a detailed digital model of New York City’s urban canopy. This model maps every tree and calculates the shading benefits they provide. By considering factors such as street orientation and building height, the tool determines the impact of each tree’s shade. The goal of this project is to help cities strategically plant and maintain trees to maximize their climate benefits and ensure equitable distribution.

The Tree Folio NYC model combines lidar sensing and tree census data to create a high-resolution, 3D representation of the city’s trees. This information can be scaled and applied to other cities using publicly available data. By understanding the shade provided by each tree, city planners and policymakers can develop strategies to mitigate extreme heat and reduce the heat island effect.

In another exciting development, Synesis One has launched its Train2Earn App ‘Workspace by Synesis’. This app allows users worldwide to train artificial intelligence (AI) and earn rewards. The app connects companies in need of high-quality AI training data with digital workers who can generate the data quickly and at competitive prices. This platform aims to create an open data supply chain to power AI applications.

Malaysia Airports Holdings and Asia Mobiliti have partnered with Google Cloud to enhance travel experiences. Malaysia Airports has migrated its digital infrastructure to Google Cloud’s open and secure platform, enabling real-time airport and flight information to be provided to passengers. Asia Mobiliti is building its mobility-as-a-service platform on Google Cloud, utilizing advanced analytics and machine learning to offer seamless multimodal transport experiences. Both companies aim to improve connectivity, sustainability, and reduce carbon emissions in the travel industry.
