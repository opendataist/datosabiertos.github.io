Study Reveals Significant Increase in ADHD Diagnoses and Prescriptions in the UK

Published: 2023-07-17

A new study conducted by researchers at UCL has shed light on the significant increase in ADHD diagnoses and prescriptions for ADHD medication in the UK over the past two decades. The study, which analyzed data from 7 million individuals aged three to 99, revealed that ADHD is more commonly diagnosed in children than in adults, and more prevalent in boys and men compared to girls and women. Interestingly, the relative increase in ADHD diagnoses and prescriptions was found to be largest among adults.

This research highlights the growing need for better support for general practitioners (GPs) in prescribing and monitoring ADHD medications. With the rising number of diagnoses and prescriptions, it is crucial that GPs receive adequate training and resources to ensure the safe and effective use of these medications. By improving the support available to GPs, we can enhance the quality of care provided to individuals with ADHD and ensure that they receive the most appropriate treatment.
