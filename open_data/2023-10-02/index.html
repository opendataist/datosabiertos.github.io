Air Pollution in India, Digital Herbarium Accessibility, AI Impact on Carnival, Veeva Systems Investment, and Nigeria's Roadmap for Prosperity

In today’s news, we bring you updates on various topics related to open data, information, and knowledge. From air pollution in India to making digital herbarium collections more accessible, and the impact of artificial intelligence on Carnival, there is a lot to explore. We also have news about investments in Veeva Systems Inc. and Nigeria’s roadmap for accelerating prosperity through technical efficiency.

Let’s start with the study conducted by the Research Institute for Humanity and Nature (RIHN) and international collaborators, which sheds light on air pollution in north-western India. Using low-cost instruments, researchers measured particulate matter (PM2.5) levels in the region, with a specific focus on the impact of crop residue burning (CRB). The results revealed that PM2.5 levels exceeded the Indian national air quality standards, reaching over 500 µg m-3 during the CRB period. This study emphasizes the importance of understanding the link between CRB and air pollution at local and regional scales through source region observations.

Moving on, the HerbUX project aims to make digital herbarium collections more accessible. Researchers from Brown and the University of Minnesota have been working on creating user-friendly interfaces that allow students and the general public to access and engage with these collections. By incorporating linked open data and extended specimen concepts, the project seeks to make online repositories more interdisciplinary. While the proposed interfaces are prototypes, the team is open to building functional and useful interfaces in the future. This initiative promotes transparency and accessibility to valuable botanical information.

In a recent symposium on the impact of artificial intelligence (AI) on Carnival, experts discussed the implications of source bias and the creation of new images using AI. Biased data in AI systems can lead to biased outputs, highlighting the importance of addressing this issue. Additionally, concerns were raised about the erosion of collaborative community creativity and the use of copyrighted images in AI-generated content. It is essential to recognize that AI is a tool, and human creativity and craft remain crucial for success. Furthermore, the lack of information about Trinidad Carnival available online affects the value of AI language models for regional users, emphasizing the need for more inclusive and diverse datasets.

Shifting gears, Prosperity Capital Advisors recently purchased a new position in Veeva Systems Inc., a cloud-based software provider for the life sciences industry. This investment reflects confidence in the company’s growth potential. Meanwhile, insiders have been selling shares of Veeva Systems, indicating a dynamic market for the company. With strong earnings results for the second quarter, Veeva Systems has garnered a “Moderate Buy” rating from analysts. This highlights the importance of analyzing statistics and records to make informed investment decisions.

Lastly, Nigeria’s Ministry of Communications, Innovation, and Digital Economy has released a roadmap titled “Accelerating our collective prosperity through technical efficiency.” The roadmap focuses on pillars such as knowledge, policy, infrastructure, innovation, entrepreneurship & capital, and trade. The plan includes ambitious targets, such as training three million technical talents, creating over 50,000 AI jobs, and achieving 70% digital literacy by 2027. The ministry aims to position Nigeria as a major player in the global technology ecosystem and improve the country’s Economic Complexity Index. This commitment to transparency and accessible information is crucial for Nigeria’s growth and development.
