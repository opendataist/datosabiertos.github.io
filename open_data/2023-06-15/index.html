Bahrain Launches Beta Version of Open Data Portal, NASA Creates Largest Open-Source PDF Archive, and More

Published: 2023-06-15

Bahrain’s Information & eGovernment Authority (iGA) has launched the Beta version of the Bahrain Open Data Portal, a platform that aims to provide access to accurate and reliable government data. This initiative is in line with Bahrain’s Economic Recovery Plan and the Government Programme, which focus on promoting transparency and encouraging innovation. The beta version of the portal offers an integrated open dataset from government entities, enhancing transparency and providing valuable information for the public, private, and academic sectors.

In another exciting development, NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) has created the largest publicly available open-source archive of PDFs. This archive, known as Safe Documents (SafeDocs), contains approximately 8 million PDFs and serves multiple purposes. Not only does it help researchers identify online threats, but it also contributes to the improvement of PDF technology. The corpus of PDFs, totaling about 8 terabytes, is hosted by the Digital Corpora project on Amazon Web Services. This initiative will be valuable for privacy researchers, software developers, and anyone interested in advancing PDF technology.

Moving on to international affairs, concerns about Chinese spying on the United States have overshadowed US Secretary of State Antony Blinken’s planned visit to China. Recent reports suggest that China has reached a deal to build a spy perch on the island of Cuba, highlighting the increasing prominence of intelligence gathering in the US-China relationship. Both countries have been expanding their intelligence capabilities, raising concerns and complicating diplomatic relations. It is crucial for the two governments to establish open communication channels to avoid faulty assumptions and promote transparency.

Shifting our focus to Saudi Arabia, the country has been making significant strides in technology and innovation. Through its Vision 2030 initiative, Saudi Arabia aims to become one of the most technologically advanced economies in the world. The government has implemented economic reforms, supported the private sector, and invested in research, development, and innovation. Initiatives such as the Saudi Vision Cable and the Saudi Data and AI Authority demonstrate the country’s commitment to technological advancement. Additionally, advancements in healthcare technology, including the digital transformation of emergency care services and the development of patient-centric apps, further showcase Saudi Arabia’s progress in the digital realm.

In the realm of data centers, BAM Digital Realty is constructing a new facility in Mumbai, India, with an expected investment of over $250 million. This data center will have a capacity of 35 megawatts, contributing to the growing demand for data center capacity in India. Mumbai, being a major data center hub in the country, offers robust power infrastructure and dense fiber connectivity. The demand for data center capacity in India is projected to reach 1,400 megawatts by 2025, highlighting the importance of such investments in supporting the country’s digital infrastructure.

Lastly, the Rising Cities. Smart Future 2023 conference will take place in Bucharest, Romania, featuring Doru Chirica, CEO of tranzy.ai, as a speaker. The conference will focus on urban development and progress, with a particular emphasis on sustainable environments and smart cities. tranzy.ai works with local authorities and public transport operators to improve mobility services through innovative tools and data-driven insights. Chirica believes that a smart city is one that balances safety, functionality, and aesthetics, utilizing technology to enhance public safety, services, and minimize environmental impact.

These developments in the world of open data, technology, and innovation highlight the importance of transparency, accessibility, and the utilization of data for the betterment of society. As we continue to progress in the digital age, it is crucial to embrace open data practices and leverage the power of information and knowledge to drive positive change.
