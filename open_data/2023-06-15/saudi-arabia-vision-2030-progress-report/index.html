Saudi Arabia Vision 2030 : Progress report

Saudi Arabia has announced its ambitious vision for the country to be one of the most technologically advanced economies in the world, which aims to boost its economy by a third of GDP in 2040. The government says it is planning to raise $22bn (22m) in investment in research, development and innovation to meet its big goals. But The BBC News Arabic looks at what it plans to achieve this years growth ambitions and how it will be able to make it more efficient and responsible than any other country on the planet, as it prepares to launch its vision of 2030, with the aim of improving its economic performance and boosting businesses, and to create an innovative society, in what would be the latest steps towards developing nations. What is it behind and what is the key to the Saudi government s vision, but what will it mean for those who want to develop their infrastructure, the government has promised to help boost the economic recovery and improve the way it deals with climate change and the impact of global warming and economic slowdowns, writes the BBC. Mohammed bin Salman, who has been appointed as chief executive of Saudi Crown Prince Abdul Aziz al-Maktoum reports from Riyadh.
