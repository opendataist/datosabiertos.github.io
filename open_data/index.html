Harnessing the Power of Data: Managing Urban Wildlife and Transforming Healthcare

Cities around the world are facing the challenge of managing urban wildlife, particularly rats. In New York City, efforts are underway to understand and control the rat population. One innovative approach is the development of a rat map, which identifies areas with high rat activity. This data is crucial in bringing about change, as seen in a previous clean-up of dirty bus stops in Santa Monica. Additionally, an app called Transit allows users to report rat sightings at subway stations, providing real-time insights to both riders and transit agencies. Manhattan has been identified as having the highest number of rat sightings, with specific subway stations being labeled as the most rat-infested.
