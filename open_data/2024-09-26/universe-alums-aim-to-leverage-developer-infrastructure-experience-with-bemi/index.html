Universe alums aim to leverage developer infrastructure experience with Bemi

Bemi, a Canadian-founded software startup based in San Francisco, has raised nearly $1.2 million in funding to enhance its product development and attract more Canadian engineering talent. The company, founded by CEO Arjun Lall and CTO Evgeny Li, aims to simplify how back-end developers utilize their databases, particularly for event-driven systems. Bemi s software integrates with Postgres, a widely-used open-source database management system, to provide a reliable audit trail for businesses. The startup s vision is to become the standard platform for tracking data changes in businesses that rely on Postgres. Lall and Li, who previously worked together at Universe and AngelList, respectively, recognized the challenge of building and maintaining database infrastructure in-house. They saw an opportunity to address this issue with Bemi, which offers automatic metadata and context for data changes. Bemi s funding round, led by Night Capital and supported by Mucker Capital, Niche Capital, Materialized View Capital, and angel investors like Monica Sarbu, brings the startup s total funding to nearly $1.2 million. The company plans to hire primarily in Canada and aims to expand its functionality to support event-driven architectures, on-premise support, and multi-region hosting for larger businesses. Bemi s focus on medium-sized and larger companies, including healthtech, climate tech, and supply and logistics, has already seen some traction. The company s developer experience, which combines application context and database events, sets it apart from competitors who may only address one aspect of the problem. In summary, Bemi is a Canadian-founded software startup that aims to streamline how back-end developers use their databases to power event-driven systems. With nearly $1.2 million in funding, the company is working to improve its infrastructure and expand its capabilities to serve medium-sized and larger businesses. Bemi s focus on providing a reliable audit trail and automatic metadata for data changes positions it as a potential solution for companies looking to optimize their database management systems.
