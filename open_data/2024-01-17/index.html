Utah Nature Center Harnesses Data to Track Migration and Support Conservation

Published: 2024-01-17

Utah Nature Center Uses Open Data to Track Migration and Help Conservation

The Ogden Nature Center in Utah now has a tracking station to learn about migration and help conservation. This station, funded by the Ogden Nature Center and Weber State University, is part of the Motus network. It collects and shares data on the migration of birds, bats, and insects.

The tracking station can detect movement up to 15 kilometers away. There are 30 tracking stations in Utah. Researchers hope to learn about the movements and needs of these species by gathering data on migration, behavior, and habitat use. This information will help protect their homes and support conservation efforts.

To improve their research, the Ogden Nature Center bands birds and attaches Motus transmitters to track different species. This extra data will give a better understanding of migration and behavior.

Open data is important in this project because it allows information to be shared openly. The Ogden Nature Center wants to work together with others to understand and protect wildlife. They make this data available to researchers and the public.
