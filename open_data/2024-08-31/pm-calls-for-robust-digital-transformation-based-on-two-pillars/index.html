PM calls for robust digital transformation based on two pillars

The government has called for a profound shift in how they serve citizens and businesses, as part of the national conference on digital transformation, which has been held in Singapore on August 31, 2018. The prime minister has urged the importance of legalisation and digitisation as key pillars of administrative procedures and public services for the public and the local authorities. () Про digital transformation is among the key priorities of digital powers, the government and localities are calling for digital infrastructure to be fully digitised, with at least 80% of all accounts processed entirely online by 2025, and increased transparency, connecting and sharing of data, to improve the quality of online services in the country, but calls for an increasing investment in digital services to meet new challenges, writes the BBC s Pham Minh Chinh at the National Committee for Digital transformation (VNA) on the coronavirus pandemic and its efforts to achieve the target of improving the digital system, saying it is essential to tackle the Covid-19 crisis and to ensure their ability to take steps towards the future of governments and services, in an attempt to boost the economic growth of internet services and improve efficiency of public service services across the world. But why is it essential to change the way the system is used to provide and use online - and how it can be done, asks an expert in computing and technology, who has said it will be the first priority in its bid to deliver significant changes to digital service improvements in 2019?
